The Way You Changed My Sky

Before you, my sky was quiet in a way that felt empty.

Not dark, not broken, just still. Nights passed without meaning, stars without names, constellations I never bothered to trace. I did not realize something was missing until you arrived so gently that I almost did not notice at first. You did not crash into my world like a storm. You came in softly, like light slowly reaching a place that had been waiting without knowing it.

You changed my sky not by being loud, but by staying.

By being consistent. By existing in my days in a way that slowly reshaped how I felt about distance, about waiting, about time itself. Suddenly, nights were no longer something to endure. They became moments filled with thoughts of you, moments where the stars felt like quiet witnesses instead of distant decorations. The universe stopped feeling so big and cold. It started feeling shared.

You gave meaning to the space between things.

To pauses in conversation. To hours apart. To silence that once felt heavy and now feels full. When I look up at the sky, I no longer feel small. I feel connected. Knowing that somewhere under that same sky you exist, breathing and dreaming, is enough to steady me when everything else feels uncertain.

You taught my heart a new way to shine.

Not burning, not overwhelming, just constant. A soft glow that does not fade with distance. Even on days when I feel tired, unsure, or lost, thinking of you rearranges everything inside me. You do not fix my world or replace it. You simply make it warmer, safer, brighter, the way stars do when you finally learn how to see them.

Now, when I imagine the future, I imagine it under a sky that looks different than it used to.

One shaped by you. One where love does not rush or disappear, where it does not need to prove itself to exist. A sky where I always know where to look when I need grounding. A sky where, no matter how far apart we are, I always know where my heart belongs.

You did not just change my sky. You gave it meaning.
